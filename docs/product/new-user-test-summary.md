# Rhythm New User Test Summary

Rhythm is a macOS planning app for church staff. It helps turn one-off tasks, recurring responsibilities, project timelines, messages, and room reservations into a clearer weekly plan.

## The Basic Idea

Rhythm is built around the way ministry work repeats. Some work is a single task, some happens every week or month, some belongs to a larger project, and some starts from outside signals like email, calendar events, or service planning. Rhythm keeps those pieces in one place so staff can see what needs attention now and what is coming next.

The main workflow is:

1. Capture work as tasks, rhythms, projects, or reservations.
2. Rhythm turns recurring work and project templates into real scheduled items.
3. Use the weekly planner and dashboard to decide what belongs in the current week.
4. Update tasks, coordinate with teammates, and reserve spaces as work changes.

## What Each Area Does

**Dashboard** gives a quick view of the week: open tasks, items due soon, active rhythms, project next steps, messages, and a quick-add task bar.

**Weekly Planner** is the main planning surface. Use it to review what is due this week and organize work into a realistic plan.

**Tasks** are individual pieces of work. Create tasks for anything that needs an owner, date, or follow-up.

**Rhythms** are recurring responsibilities. Instead of remembering to recreate the same work every week or month, create a rhythm and Rhythm generates the upcoming task instances.

**Projects** are repeatable multi-step efforts. A project template can break larger work into steps scheduled around a target date, such as tasks due several weeks before an event.

**Messages** lets staff communicate in Rhythm without losing the planning context. Unread message counts appear in the sidebar.

**Facilities** lets staff view spaces and create reservations. Regular users can manage their own reservations; facilities managers can reserve on behalf of others and manage broader schedules. Single and recurring reservations are supported.

**Automations and Integrations** connect Rhythm to outside context. Google setup enables Gmail and Calendar signals so planning context can flow into Rhythm while Rhythm remains the source of truth for work planning.

## What To Try During Testing

Start by signing in with Google and completing any Google permission prompt. Then try these core flows:

1. Add a task from the Dashboard, give it a due date, and confirm it appears in Tasks or the Weekly Planner.
2. Create or review a rhythm and check whether its upcoming work is easy to understand.
3. Open Projects and look at how project steps are organized around dates.
4. Use the Weekly Planner to review what belongs in the current week.
5. Send or read a message and confirm the sidebar unread count behaves as expected.
6. Create a facility reservation with a room, date, time, requester, and notes.
7. Quit and reopen Rhythm, then confirm your session and recent work are still there.

## Feedback We Need

As you test, focus on whether Rhythm matches how you actually plan work:

- Is it obvious where to put new work?
- Are tasks, rhythms, and projects distinct enough?
- Does the Dashboard help you know what to do next?
- Does the Weekly Planner make the week feel more manageable?
- Is facilities booking clear enough to use without training?
- Did anything feel slow, confusing, missing, or unreliable?

Please share the exact screen, what you expected to happen, and what happened instead. Screenshots are helpful when something looks wrong or a workflow is hard to follow.
